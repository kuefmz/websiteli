# Websiteli Project

This document is the high-level product and business context for Websiteli. Future work should also read `ARCHITECTURE.md`, `CONTRIBUTING.md`, `ANALYTICS.md`, `LOCALIZATION.md`, and `TESTING.md` before changing code.

## Vision

Websiteli is a Swiss-based website, SEO, analytics, and automation service for small businesses. The site exists to turn Websiteli's own digital presence into a lead-generation system and to demonstrate the same standards Websiteli sells to clients: clear ownership, fast static pages, localized content, search visibility, measurable conversions, and practical automation.

## Business Goals

- Generate qualified consultation requests through the contact form.
- Capture newsletter subscribers who may become future leads.
- Explain website packages and pricing without requiring a sales call for basic context.
- Demonstrate credibility through live demos, illustrative project analyses, technical SEO, and ownership-focused content.
- Preserve attribution from UTM/click-id campaigns so marketing efforts can be evaluated.

## Target Audience

- Small businesses and local service providers.
- Restaurants, cafes, consultants, coaches, freelancers, shops, and SMEs.
- Businesses in Switzerland and international markets that need a professional website, SEO foundation, lead capture, analytics, or automation.
- Prospects who may currently rely on social media, LinkedIn, an AI-generated site, or an outdated website.

## Unique Value Proposition

Websiteli is not positioned as a quick AI website generator. It combines:

- Professional static websites.
- SEO and Google visibility foundations.
- Analytics and attribution tracking.
- Lead capture and conversion paths.
- Client ownership of domain, hosting, content, analytics, and source assets.
- Optional practical AI and business automation.

## Branding

- Brand name: `Websiteli` / `websiteli.ch`.
- Visual style: clean, business-focused, restrained, practical, Swiss-based, conversion-oriented.
- Primary brand color is red (`--brand` in `src/styles/global.css`), supported by neutral surfaces and limited accent colors.
- Tone: practical, clear, helpful, ownership-focused, not hype-driven.

## Design Principles

- Build the actual usable experience, not marketing filler.
- Prefer clear sections, stable layouts, responsive grids, and direct CTAs.
- Maintain accessibility basics: semantic headings, labels, live regions for forms, keyboard-friendly links/buttons.
- Keep pages static and fast where possible.
- Avoid adding dependencies unless there is a clear benefit.
- Preserve existing localized routing, SEO metadata, analytics, and conversion flows.

## Conversion Goals

Primary conversions:

- Contact form submission.
- Consultation CTA click.
- Package CTA click.

Secondary conversions:

- Newsletter subscription.
- WhatsApp/email click.
- Demo click.
- Blog share click.

Conversion components and flows are documented in `FEATURES.md` and `ANALYTICS.md`.

## SEO Strategy

- Static Astro pages with localized URLs.
- Canonical URLs and `hreflang` alternates.
- Organization, WebSite, LocalBusiness, Service, BlogPosting, Breadcrumb, and FAQ structured data.
- Sitemap generated by `@astrojs/sitemap`.
- Blog content targeting business website, SEO, analytics, ownership, AI, automation, and local business terms.
- Internal links from home to services/pricing, portfolio, blog, contact, demos, and related article pages.

## Lead Generation Strategy

- Homepage introduces offer and routes users toward services/pricing, portfolio, and contact.
- Services/pricing page presents package options and price ranges.
- Portfolio combines live demo and illustrative business analyses.
- Blog articles include inline, bottom, sidebar, and mobile CTAs.
- Forms post to a Google Apps Script endpoint and include pricing, attribution, privacy consent, and source metadata.

## Current Roadmap

## Non-Goals

- Do not turn the site into a generic AI website generator.
- Do not add a user-facing market selector; pricing is detected automatically.
- Do not expose unpublished content ideas as public roadmap content.
- Do not replace the static Astro architecture without a strong reason.
- Do not remove attribution, pricing metadata, privacy consent, structured data, or localized routing.
